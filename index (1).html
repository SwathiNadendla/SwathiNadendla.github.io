About

My path to computational biology hasn't been traditional, and that's precisely what gives me a unique perspective. Starting with a Bachelor's in Pharmacy, then diving deep into Pharmacoinformatics for my Master's degree, I discovered my passion for computational approaches during my industry experience as a data analyst in respiratory healthcare.

Each transition taught me something fundamental about how data can tell stories about living systems. My time analyzing market trends and competitor activities in the pharmaceutical industry sharpened my ability to extract actionable insights from complex datasets—a skill that translates beautifully to genomic analysis.

Today, as a PhD candidate in Dr. El-Gazzar's laboratory at Iowa State University's Veterinary Diagnostic Laboratory, I focus on developing computational approaches for avian pathogen diagnostics. My work with Newcastle Disease Virus (NDV) and Avian Metapneumovirus (AMPV) detection using Oxford Nanopore sequencing combines everything I love: complex data, biological insight, and practical applications that matter for animal health.

What drives me is the challenge of making sense of complex genomic datasets and translating that understanding into tools that can make a real difference. Whether I'm designing multiplex PCR panels, developing real-time analysis pipelines, or mentoring fellow students, my goal is always the same: clarity, rigor, and impact.

Research Focus

Developing computational approaches that bridge the gap between complex biological data and actionable insights for infectious disease monitoring and precision medicine.

Viral Diagnostics

Designing multiplex PCR panels and real-time analysis pipelines for Oxford Nanopore sequencing of avian pathogens. Current focus on Newcastle Disease Virus and Avian Metapneumovirus detection and characterization.

Pipeline Development

Building scalable, portable bioinformatic pipelines for genomic sequencing data analysis. Developing tools that can run on limited computational resources while maintaining high accuracy and speed.

Precision Medicine

Applying genomics and systems biology approaches to advance drug discovery and personalized treatment strategies. Bridging computational biology with translational medicine applications.
