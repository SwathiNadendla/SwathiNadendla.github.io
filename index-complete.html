The Journey

From Molecules to Microbes

My path to computational biology wasn't linear‚Äîit was intentional.

Starting with a Bachelor's in Pharmacy in India, I developed a fascination with how drugs interact with biological systems. This curiosity led me to NIPER Mohali, where I earned my Master's in Pharmacoinformatics, bridging the gap between pharmaceutical sciences and computational approaches.

At Course5i in Bangalore, I analyzed real-world clinical data in the respiratory disease space, extracting insights from messy datasets and understanding how data drives decision-making in healthcare markets. This experience crystallized something important: the future of medicine lies in our ability to decode biological information at scale.

Today, as a PhD candidate at Iowa State University in Dr. El-Gazzar's laboratory, I'm developing computational approaches for avian pathogen diagnostics. My work focuses on Newcastle Disease Virus and Avian Metapneumovirus detection using Oxford Nanopore sequencing‚Äîdesigning multiplex PCR panels, building real-time analysis pipelines, and creating tools that turn raw sequencing data into actionable diagnostic information.

Beyond the bench and the terminal, I'm committed to building community. As President of the BCB Graduate Student Organization and Co-Director of the Interdisciplinary Biological Sciences Symposium, I work to create spaces where diverse scientific perspectives converge and collaboration thrives.
